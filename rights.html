THE PROTECTION OF CHILD FROM SEXUAL OFFENCES ACT (2012)

"The POCSO Act stands for Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012. It is a comprehensive law that aims to protect children from sexual abuse, exploitation and pornography in India
The POCSO Act is a gender-neutral law, meaning it applies to both boys and girls who are below the age of 18 years2. It also does not discriminate between the perpetrators of child sexual abuse based on their gender2. The POCSO Act defines various forms of sexual offences against children, such as penetrative sexual assault, aggravated penetrative sexual assault, sexual assault, aggravated sexual assault, sexual harassment, and using a child for pornographic purposes1. It also prescribes strict punishments for these offences, ranging from imprisonment for 3 years to life imprisonment, and fine.

JUVENILE JUSTICE ACT (2015)

"The Juvenile Act is a law that deals with children who are in conflict with the law or in need of care and protection in India. Here are some of the main points about the Juvenile Act: The Juvenile Act was first enacted in 1986 and then amended in 2000 and 2015. The latest amendment was in 2018, which aimed to streamline the adoption process and empower the district magistrates to oversee the functioning of various agencies under the Act1. The Juvenile Act defines a child as anyone who is below the age of 18 years. However, it also allows for the trial of a child as an adult if he or she is accused of committing a heinous offence, such as rape or murder, and is found to be mature enough to understand the consequences of the act by a Juvenile Justice Board. RIGHT TO HEALTH

"The right to health care for children is a fundamental component of international human rights law. The Convention on the Rights of the Child, adopted by the United Nations General Assembly, emphasizes the right of children to access essential health care services. Additionally, the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and various international legal instruments enshrine the right of children to the highest attainable standard of physical and mental health. This right underlines the importance of providing children with access to adequate health care, including preventive, curative, and rehabilitative services, with a focus on ensuring their well-being and development. International laws and declarations prioritize children's rights to protection, education, and health care, signifying the global commitment to safeguarding the well-being of every child."

RIGHT TO EXPRESSION

"The Right to Expression is a fundamental human right enshrined in various international and national laws. It encompasses the freedom to express one's opinions, ideas, and beliefs without censorship or restraint. This right includes the freedom of speech, press, assembly, and association, allowing individuals to participate in public discourse, criticize government actions, and advocate for social change. In India, the Right to Expression is protected under Article 19 of the Constitution, subject to reasonable restrictions in the interest of sovereignty, security, public order, decency, or morality."

GOOD TOUCH BAD TOUCH ACT

"Good Touch: Good touch is any physical contact that makes you feel safe, happy, and comfortable. Examples of good touches include hugs from family and friends, high fives, and handshakes. It's important to remember that good touches should always happen with permission and should never make you feel uncomfortable.
Bad touch is any physical contact that makes you feel scared, confused, or uncomfortable. Examples of bad touches include someone touching your private parts in a way that makes you feel uneasy or scared, or someone trying to keep a touch a secret from you or others. It's important to know that it's okay to say no to a bad touch and to tell a trusted adult about it."

STRANGERS ARE DANGER

"Stranger danger refers to the potential risks and dangers children may face when encountering unknown individuals. Not all strangers are harmful, but teaching kids to differentiate between safe and potentially dangerous interactions is crucial.
Child abduction and abuse are serious issues globally. Approximately 800,000 children go missing in the United States each year. While not all cases involve stranger abduction, educating kids about potential risks is vital.
Educating children about stranger danger empowers them to make informed choices. By fostering open communication and awareness, we can help our kids stay safe. Remember, their well-being is our priority!"
